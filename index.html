About me

I am an Assistant Professor of Mathematics at Hampton University. My research interests lie in functional analysis and operator theory, with a focus on Banach space geometry, numerical index theory, Bishop–Phelps–Bollobás type properties, tensor product spaces, and related questions in operator theory.

Education

I completed my Ph.D. in Mathematics at the University of Memphis under the guidance of Fernanda Botelho and Bentuo Zheng. I received my Master of Science in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay and my undergraduate degree in Mathematics from Delhi University.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of functional analysis, operator theory, and the geometry of Banach spaces. I am particularly interested in numerical index theory, Bishop–Phelps–Bollobás type properties, quotient lifting properties, tensor product spaces, and algebraic and spectral properties of operators.
